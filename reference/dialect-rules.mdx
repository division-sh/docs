The analyzer enforces a strict contract dialect. These rules are the ones authors most often
trip on; the analyzer reports each as a `dialect_compliance` or related finding.

## Structural rules

<Steps>
  <Step title="Guards use an object, never a bare string">
    A guard is `{id, check, on_fail}`. A bare string check is invalid.
  </Step>
  <Step title="on_complete is an ordered list">
    `on_complete` is a YAML list, never a map. Conditions are evaluated top to bottom; the
    first match wins.
  </Step>
  <Step title="on_complete and rules are mutually exclusive">
    A handler uses one or the other, never both.
  </Step>
  <Step title="create_entity and accumulate cannot coexist">
    A handler that mints an entity cannot also accumulate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="One entity-acquisition mode per handler">
    Declare exactly one of `create_entity`, `select_entity`, `select_or_create_entity`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="advances_to is a single string">
    Not a list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="A handler top-level emit needs a single emit site">
    If the handler also declares `rules`, `on_complete`, `accumulate.on_timeout`, or
    `fan_out` emit, a top-level `emit` is ambiguous and fails boot.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Field and value rules

- **Conditions are prefixed.** Every CEL condition begins with `payload.`, `entity.`,
  `policy.`, `accumulated.`, or `fan_out.`.
- **`data_accumulation.writes` uses the canonical forms.** Direct (`field`), mapped
  (`{source_field, target_field}`), literal (`{target_field, value}`), or computed
  (`{target_field, expression}`). `value` and `expression` never coexist; the constant key is
  `value`, not `literal`.
- **`source_event` is the canonical key**, not `source`.
- **`clear_gates: true` clears all gates.** Selective clearing is not supported.
- **Events declare flat payloads.** Fields go directly under the event name; the `payload:`
  wrapper is retired.
- **Entity fields live in `entities.yaml`.** The retired `package.yaml.entity_schema` is not
  accepted.

## Retired fields

`emits`, `payload_transform`, `fan_out.emit_per_item`, and `fan_out.emit_mapping` are retired.
See [Handler fields](/reference/handler-fields) for replacements.
